Barcelona Contamination Dataviz

This study comprises the analysis of the air quality of Barcelona. The source of the data analyzed is the Barcelona's City Hall Open Data Service.
The aim of this project is to analyze the trends and tendencies of different air pollutants. An interesting pattern observed is that NO2 pollution follows the overall human activity during the day: it is low at night and lunch time and is high at rush hours. The map shows the locations of the measuring stations.

The world gets hotter and more crowded, our engines continue to pump out dirty emissions: the air we breathe is becoming polluted. Air pollution has serious effects on health and it generally lowers our quality of life and life expectancy.

Climate change is closely linked to air pollution. How? The main driver of climate change is fossil fuel combustion which is, at the same time, a major contributor to air pollution. Taking measures on one, can help to mitigate the other.

Nitrogen dioxide (NO2)

The major sources of NO2 emissions are combustion processes (heating, power generation, and fuel combustion in engines in vehicles).

Ozone (O3)

Is one of the main compounds of the photochemical smog. It is formed by a reaction: sunlight + nitrogen oxide (like NO2). Thus, the highest levels of O3 pollution occur during periods of sunny weather.

Particulate Matter (PM10)

A mix of small particles of dust, ashes, cement and soot coming from fuel combustion and road traffic.

The measures were taken several times by hour and then the mean value of those values was given. However, after 04/04/2019, the source of the data is the Public Health Agency of Barcelona (ASPB), which at each hour measures the exact value at that time. Thus, the scales are different and we cannot compare the plots from those two different data sources, yet we can observe the trends.
